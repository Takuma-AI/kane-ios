Why This Matters Now
Saturday's farmers market is our first major public touchpoint before grand reopening. When people say "I'll check it out," they often come by within 48 hours of hearing about us. The space needs to deliver on the promise we make at the booth.
The real fear: People walk in, see an unfinished space that feels like "just another fitness studio" and never come back. First impressions are everything in Winnetka - this community talks.
The contractor finishes tomorrow (Friday), but finishing construction isn't the same as creating an experience. We need the space to tell our story: elevated, intentional, different from anything else in the North Shore.
Success Indicators
• People pause when they walk in (the "wow" moment)
• The bathroom makes them do a double-take
• They notice and ask about the triangle wall
• The entry flow feels natural, not cluttered
• Instagram-worthy moments exist without staging
Critical Context for Execution
Space constraints: 12x12 main room, narrow entry corridor. Every inch matters.

Brand tension: Corporate wants gray walls and standard fixtures. We want warmth and personality. Threading the needle between compliance and uniqueness.

Time reality: Whatever isn't done by 6pm Friday waits until after farmers market. No second chances for first impressions.

Farmers market materials ready to convert
Target: Friday night • Enables: 50+ grand reopening attendees
Why This Matters Now
2,000 people flow through Winnetka Farmers Market on Saturday mornings. These aren't random passersby - they're engaged community members who value local businesses and have Saturday morning energy.
The opportunity: Parents are there with kids who need activities. They're in "yes" mode, credit cards in hand, looking for what's new in town. This is our highest-quality lead pool before grand reopening.
But farmers markets are sensory overload. We have 5 seconds to catch attention, 30 seconds to create interest, and 2 minutes max to convert to action. Every material needs to work hard.
Success Indicators
• 50+ people take flyers and seem genuinely interested
• 20+ kids sign up for Olympics on the spot
• Parents ask "Is this safe for beginners?" (engagement!)
• We capture email/phone for follow-up
• People take photos of our booth/demo
Critical Context for Materials
Audience reality: Affluent parents who've "tried everything" for their kids. Skeptical of new fitness trends but desperate for activities that build confidence.

Competitive context: They know yoga, pilates, barre. They DON'T know aerial can be accessible. We're fighting the "circus = dangerous" perception.

Conversion psychology: Kids Olympics is less threatening than "aerial classes." It's time-bound, competitive (parents love achievement), and sounds fun not scary.
Shots on Goal
Design "safe adventure" focused flyer
Lead with Kids Olympics (Sept 14, ages 6-14, beginner-friendly). Include grand reopening schedule. QR code to signup form. Bright, not intimidating.
Tonight
Create 60-second demo routine
Accessible moves that make people think "I could do that!" Not cirque-level intimidation. Include one kid if possible - social proof.
Critical
Set up simple email capture system
iPad with Google Form: Name, email, "Kid age" or "Adult interest", follow-up permission. Prize drawing for motivation (free month?).
Tonight
Prep elevator pitches for common concerns
"Is it safe?" → "Safer than playground monkey bars, all certified instructors." "Is my kid too young?" → "We start at 6, builds confidence like nothing else."
Friday
Print 200 flyers at FedEx
Full color, cardstock not paper (quality signal). Include map to studio from market. Friday afternoon to avoid rush.
